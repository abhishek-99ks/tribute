Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug, an American agronomist

Life of Dr. Norman Borlaug

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Lowa
  • 1933 - Left the family's farm and attended "University of Minnesota"
  • 1935 - To meet the finances of studies, he put hold on the education periodically to earn some income
  • 1937 - Recieved 'Bachelor of Science' in forestry and joined United States Forest Service
  • 1937 - Married Margret Gibson. Returns to school study under Stakman, who taught him about breeding pest-resistant plants
  • 1945 - Discovers a way to grow wheat twice each season
  • 1970 - Recieved the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1983 - Helped African countries dramatically increase their maize and sorghum yields
  • 1984 - Became professor at Texas A&M University
  • 2005 - Stated, "We will have to double the world food supply by 2050". Believed that genetically modified organisms (GMO) was the only way to increase food production as the world runs out of unused arable land. Said that, "We've been genetically modifying plants and animals for a long time. Long before we called it science, people were selecting the best breeds"
  • 2009 - Died at the age of 95

"Borlaug's life and achievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution that one man's towering intellect, persistence and scientific vision can make to human peace and progress."
